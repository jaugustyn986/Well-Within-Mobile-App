# Template: Privacy Trust Post

## Content Job

Trust or privacy post.

## Best Used When

- The goal is trust, profile visits, follows, or saves.
- The account needs to differentiate Well Within from generic cycle trackers.
- The audience is likely privacy-conscious about fertility or health data.

## Target Metric

Primary: profile visits and follows.  
Secondary: saves.

## Source Patterns

- Privacy-first product positioning.
- Trust proof paired with a clear user benefit.
- Calm boundary-setting around personal health information.

## Format Options

- Single feed image.
- 4-6 slide carousel.
- Story with link sticker if the goal is traffic.

## Slide Structure

1. Privacy-first hook.
2. Name why cycle notes are personal.
3. Explain the local-first or user-owned benefit in plain language.
4. Connect privacy to calmer charting.
5. Optional product-light CTA.

## Visual System

Default to a quiet journal-led privacy visual: paper, phone-on-desk, notebook, or personal routine context. Use app-led framing only if the post needs a specific product privacy proof.

Avoid locks, sirens, scary data imagery, or fear-based copy. Privacy should feel quiet and respectful.

## Copy Patterns

- "Your cycle notes deserve privacy, too."
- "Charting is personal. Your data should feel personal, too."
- "Private by design, because fertility charting is not generic data."

## Caption Pattern

Hook -> privacy value -> exact CTA.

Use "link in bio" only if the post is intentionally product-light or direct CTA.

## Safety Notes

- Do not make unsupported security claims.
- Do not mention private user data or show realistic personal health details.
- Keep claims aligned with the current product behavior: data stays on device unless backup is chosen.
