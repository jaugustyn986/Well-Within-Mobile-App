# Template: Educational Carousel

## Content Job

Educational charting post.

## Best Used When

- The goal is saves or shares.
- The audience needs a simple distinction, vocabulary, or charting concept.
- Metrics are unclear and the account needs to become easier to understand from a cold first impression.

## Target Metric

Primary: saves and shares.  
Secondary: follows and profile visits.

## Source Patterns

- Question-led education from larger cycle education accounts.
- Charting-first education from fertility-awareness accounts.
- One idea per slide from high-performing carousel formats.

## Slide Structure

Think holistically, but **anchor on slide 1** (hook + visual system), then repeat typography and layout patterns on later slides.

**Full arc (up to ~7 slides)** when needed:

1. Clear first-frame hook with the main keyword.
2. Name the common confusion.
3. Explain the charting idea in plain language.
4. Give 2-3 observable words or examples.
5. Add a calm boundary or contrast.
6. Summarize the value.
7. Save/follow CTA.

**Shorter arc (often 4–5 slides):** combine steps—e.g. hook → confusion + idea → boundary → CTA—or drop merge steps so the deck stays tight without losing clarity.

## Visual System

Default to journal-led education: warm paper, notebook, calendar, desk, bedside, or morning-light context with restrained typography and one idea per slide.

Use soft explainer sketches only when they make the charting concept clearer than photo or texture support. Avoid boxed card systems as the default look.

## Copy Patterns

- "Cycle tracking and fertility charting are not always the same thing."
- "5 words that make fertility charting easier."
- "What fertility charting can and cannot tell you."
- "Observation-based charting starts with what you notice."

## Caption Pattern

Hook -> value -> save/follow CTA.

Mention Well Within lightly only if it feels natural. For reach/trust posts, the save/follow CTA should come before link-in-bio.

## Safety Notes

- Do not dismiss period tracking.
- Do not imply prediction, diagnosis, contraception effectiveness, or pregnancy outcomes.
- Use neutral terms: observation, sensation, appearance, peak day, pattern over time.
- Avoid proprietary method affiliation language.
